Moscow Ultrasound Lab

Medical Ultrasound Imaging Research Group @ Moscow Center for Diagnostics and Telemedicine
Moscow Center for Diagnostics and Telemedicine • Moscow Power Engineering Institute • Russian Academy of Sciences

We develop end‑to‑end solutions for ultrasound imaging — from open‑source C++ frameworks (RUFUS) and tissue‑mimicking phantoms to augmented reality navigation for ultrasound‑guided interventions. Our goal is to make ultrasound research more reproducible, training more effective, and interventions more precise.

🔬 Research Projects

🔬 RUFUS
Open‑source C++ framework for ultrasound signal processing on real RF data.
RF → Beamforming → Image → AR
🧪 Anthropomorphic Phantoms
Tissue‑mimicking models for ultrasound training and algorithm validation.
3D‑printed · Validated · Open STL
🥽 Augmented Reality
AR navigation for ultrasound‑guided interventions and medical education.
Real‑time overlay · Needle tracking

🤝 What we offer to partners & collaborators

  • ✔ Open‑source C++ framework (RUFUS) for research & prototyping
  • ✔ Custom tissue‑mimicking phantoms (3D‑printed, clinically validated)
  • ✔ AR navigation modules for ultrasound‑guided interventions
  • ✔ Open datasets (raw RF signals, phantom designs, validation data)
  • ✔ Joint publications, reproducibility packages, and student supervision

Need something else? Just ask — we love challenging tasks.

📂 Open Datasets & 3D‑Printable Phantoms

🧽 Spongy Ultrasound Training Phantom
3D‑printed porous medium (156 µm fibers) with anechoic cysts. Mimics muscle/breast tissue. Withstands 300+ punctures.
Open-source 3h 23min print

💀 Aberration Correction — Raw RF Dataset
Raw RF data for phase aberration correction using Legendre polynomials. Includes C++ code and ex vivo temporal bone data.
⭐ Key paper Synthetic aperture
📄 Paper: Leonov et al., Medical Physics 2024 →
